Biomaterials testing is not only about safety. It is also about whether a material produces the intended biological function under realistic conditions. Researchers evaluate surface reactivity, dissolution behavior, and cellular response to understand mechanisms, not just outcomes.

The materials side is crucial because biological response is sensitive to surface chemistry, roughness, porosity, and ion release. Two materials with similar bulk composition can behave very differently if their surfaces are processed differently. That is why characterization and processing history are inseparable from biological interpretation.

Researchers also care about reproducibility. A promising result that cannot be reproduced across batches or processing runs is not a robust materials solution. Testing therefore includes not only biological assays but also careful control and documentation of materials fabrication and surface state.
